Frequently Asked Questions

What is this project about?
Maricopa County Department of Public Health (MCDPH) is working with community partners and residents to make it easier to get to the places people visit in Sunnyslope for food, fun, and services. We want to gather feedback from residents about their awareness and use of the SMART Circulator, the free neighborhood mini-bus, to access services in the area. We want to know what they love about the Circulator, and what they find to be a challenge. Information will be collected through a survey as well as other methods, such as field trips by residents on the Circulator.

What is the SMART Circulator?
SMART is a free neighborhood circulator service provided by Phoenix Public Transit. Mini-buses travel an 8-mile route in Sunnyslope both directions every 35 minutes. Travel time from one end to the other is approximately 45 minutes. On major streets, residents wait at designated bus stops. On neighborhood streets, residents flag down the mini-bus to have it stop.

How will the survey be implemented?
Resident Ambassadors will assist in collecting information from fellow residents. Surveys will be available in select locations and Resident Ambassadors will help spread the word and encourage participation.

When will the survey take place?
The survey process will take place December 2018 to February 2019. Surveys will be available to residents in and around select community sites (food bank, two apartment complexes, community/senior center and community garden).

What is the role of a Resident Ambassador?
Resident Ambassadors are individuals that have been selected through an application process to help “be the voice” of their fellow community residents. In addition to helping with survey efforts, Resident Ambassadors will participate in field trips on the Circulator, including walking between stops and select destinations to provide additional information related to using the service. They also will gain confidence and leadership skills through trainings.

How will this project help residents of our community?
Resident input and participation is essential to this project! Feedback gathered may be used to work towards changes in Circulator routes and schedules, and planning for walking routes between stops. This project is the first, and will be repeated in different communities throughout the Valley.  So your work as an RA will be helping people for years to come! Helping residents better use the Circulator to navigate the neighborhood could improve their health and quality of life.

What local organizations are supporting this effort?
MCDPH is working with City of Phoenix Public Transit Department, Valley Metro, HonorHealth Desert Mission, Sunnyslope Manor, Café Quetzal, Keep Phoenix Beautiful Mountain View Park Community Garden, and Sunnyslope Senior/Community Center to reach local residents.

Who is funding this project?
Access Sunnyslope is a project made possible by funding from the Vitalyst Health Foundation’s Live Well Arizona. The Live Well Arizona partners are on a mission to connect, lift up and celebrate the great statewide work taking place to develop healthier, more livable communities in Arizona. At its core, the effort starts with the notion that embracing shared understanding and building collective momentum can enable all involved to reach a higher level of success.
